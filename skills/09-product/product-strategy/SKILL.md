# Product Strategy Skill

Comprehensive product strategy including vision, roadmap, positioning, and product-market fit.

## When to Use This Skill

USE THIS SKILL when:
- Creating product strategy
- Building product roadmap
- Defining product vision
- Product positioning
- Product-market fit analysis
- "What's our product strategy?"
- Prioritizing features
- Building roadmap

DO NOT USE for:
- Technical implementation (use engineering-suite)
- User research (use user-research)
- Product analytics (use product-analytics)

## Product Strategy Framework

### 1. Product Vision

**Vision Template:**

```markdown
## Product Vision

### Vision Statement
In [TIMEFRAME], [COMPANY] will be the [POSITION] for [TARGET], enabling [OUTCOME] through [DIFFERENTIATOR].

[Example]
In 2028, Acme will be the leading AI-powered customer support platform for mid-market SaaS companies, enabling 10x faster resolution times through intelligent automation.

### Vision Elements
| Element | Current State | Desired State |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| [Element 1] | [Current] | [Target] |
| [Element 2] | [Current] | [Target] |
```

### 2. Product Mission

**Mission Template:**
```markdown
## Product Mission

[COMPANY]'s product exists to [PROBLEM] for [CUSTOMER], so they can [OUTCOME].

[Example]
Acme Support exists to eliminate customer support frustration for SaaS founders, so they can focus on building great products.
```

### 5. Competitive Positioning

**Positioning Statement:**

```markdown
## Positioning Statement

For [TARGET CUSTOMER] who [NEED], [PRODUCT] is [CATEGORY] that [BENEFIT], unlike [COMPETITOR], our product [DIFFERENTIATOR].

[Example]
For SaaS founders who need to provide great customer support without hiring a support team, Acme is an AI-powered support platform that solves tickets automatically, unlike Zendesk, which requires human agents for every query.
```

## Roadmap Planning

### Roadmap Types

| Type | Purpose | Timeframe | Audience |
|------|---------|----------|----------|
| Strategic | High-level themes | 12+ months | Board, investors |
| Annual | Feature commitments | 12 months | Company, customers |
| Quarterly | Detailed execution | 3 months | Team |
| Sprint | Week-by-week | 2 weeks | Team |

### Prioritization Framework

**RICE Scoring:**

```markdown
## Feature Prioritization: RICE

### Scoring Template
| Feature | Reach | Impact | Confidence | Effort | Total |
|---------|-------|--------|-------------|--------|-------|
| [Feature 1] | [X/10] | [X/3] | [X% /3] | [X/5] | [Total/10] |
| [Feature 2] | [X/10] | [X/3] | [X% /3] | [X/5] | [Total/10] |

### Score Interpretation
- 40-50: Must build
- 25-39: Should build
- 10-24: Could build
- <10: Won't build this quarter

### Prioritization Criteria
| Criteria | Weight | Definition |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Reach | 25% | How many customers? |
| Impact | 30% | How much value? |
| Confidence | 25% | How sure are we? |
| Effort | 20% | How hard? (reverse scored) |
```

### Quarterly Planning Process

```
Week 1: Review last quarter
Week 2: Inputs (customer, data, team)
Week 3: Theme selection
Week 4: Detailed planning
```
